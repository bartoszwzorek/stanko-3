Permanent Make-up

Permanent make-up is an alternative to traditional make-up. In the case of eyebrows, the procedure is aimed at shaping them, getting rid of irregularities of superciliary arches, correcting eyebrow asymmetry – if it is not too large. If you struggle with very thin hair, if there are gaps between your hairs or if you are simply dissatisfied with the look of your eyebrows – permanent make-up is exactly for you.

Permanent make-up is to emphasise the beauty of clients so that it is in harmony with the natural features of their faces, and to create a lasting beautifying effect.

As a linergist I know well that we must deal with many things every day and we do not always have enough time to put on an ideal make-up. Impeccable make-up makes us feel attractive and self-confident. Permanent make-up not only helps us save time every day but it also guarantees that we will look beautiful in each situation, at any time of day or night.

Ombre Brows

Ombre Brows is a technique thanks to which we can obtain the effect of so-called shading. Unlike other pigmentation techniques, it is suitable for each skin type, age and looks. By making use of the right method of pigmentation we can spread the colour in such a way that the make-up will look very natural. With the Ombre Brows technique we can highlight eyebrows with a subtle, medium or intense effect, depending on mutual arrangements.

When choosing a subtle effect – pigment is injected into the skin very shallowly. As a result, the make-up is not intense and less durable.

With a stronger effect, pigment is placed a bit deeper and more passages are made in a given segment, which makes the make-up more durable.

If you want the effect to be more intense and long-lasting, have the make-up done more often. I do not recommend deciding on too deep pigmentation to achieve satisfying effects already after the first procedure. It is good to know that too deep pigmentation may result in a greyish tint after healing or an effect of unattractive tattoo. Personally, I am against such practices. I believe that such pigmentation will not highlight but rather exaggerate our looks in an unaesthetic way.

Each of us has great reserves of beauty. It is sometimes enough to emphasise our good looks in a skilful and subtle way to bring out their natural glamour.
