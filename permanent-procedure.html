Everything you always wanted to know about permanent make-up.

What is the procedure about?

The permanent make-up procedure consists in injecting pigment between epidermis and dermis with the use of a needle and a special device.

As permanent make-up breaks the continuity of epidermis, I pay great attention to hygiene at work and observe appropriate washing and disinfection procedures. I use disposable needles, which I open right before the procedure in the presence of my client.

Service quality and safety of my clients are very important to me, which is why I perform the procedure with the professional Quantum device, designed for aesthetic pigmentation. Selecting the right equipment guarantees high-quality pigmentation and the comfort of painless procedure.

Consultations

Since permanent make-up is an invasive procedure with a long-lasting effect, I would like you to understand it well.

During the consultation we discuss the technique, shape and pigment colour. Then, I prepare initial visualisation by making a preliminary drawing.

The shape of eyebrows depends on the natural structure of the superciliary arch, frontal bone, space between eyes, general appearance of the face, temples and the number of natural hairs.

I select the pigment individually, depending on the colour of skin, eyes, natural looks of the client and her preferences.

The consultation is one of the most important stages of the whole procedure. It is extremely important to me to determine mutual expectations regarding the effects of permanent make-up. I approach each client individually. I try to dedicate as much time as needed to each client. It is of great significance to me to make each of you feel comfortable and safe, which is why I do my best to answer all questions and dispel all doubts you may have before the start of the procedure.

The first stage

Permanent eyebrow make-up is done in two stages. Pigmentation is preceded with a consultation during which we determine the shape of the eyebrows and the tone of pigment.

The permanent make-up procedure starts with disinfection and thorough cleansing of the skin. When the initial drawing and mutual arrangements are accepted I proceed to the first stage of pigmentation.

Correction

The second meeting takes place 5 weeks to 3 months from the first pigmentation. Only the second procedure guarantees the dream effect. It is aimed at strengthening and reinforcing the first pigmentation. Without the correction the procedure is not finished and the satisfying effect is not achieved. It is worth setting aside some time and booking a convenient date for the second appointment.

It sometimes happens that the pigment is absorbed in full already after the first meeting and it may seem that correction is not needed. However, I schedule with my clients the second meeting to check the quality and effects of pigmentation, and to refine the smallest details. I try to do each make-up with maximum accuracy. I know that paying attention to the tiniest details, concentration and precision are the guarantee of perfect make-up and satisfying effects.
